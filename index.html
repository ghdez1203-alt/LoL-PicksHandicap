Click a team to expand its roster. Season and recent per-game kill averages are editable — tune them as real numbers come in, and the picks engine will use your values.

How the projection works: each player has a per-game kill average multiplied by the number of maps in the combo. The matchup strength adjustment is the key one: every team has a strength rating (my read from MSI power rankings and results), so when you give an opponent, a favorite's kills trend up while a heavy underdog's inflated domestic numbers get suppressed — the ranking reflects who's actually likely to win, not just raw averages. Recent form, head-to-head, and ban-rate risk apply smaller nudges. The adjusted projection is compared to your line to pick a side, and confidence reflects how likely that side clears using a Poisson kill distribution. No live game simulation is run — it's a direct statistical projection.

Limitations: the per-game numbers and team ratings are my hand-compiled estimates, not a licensed feed, and are roughest for the LCP and CBLOL teams. The matchup adjustment is a heuristic, not a prediction engine — it can't know the draft, form on the day, or variance. Map 2, Map 3, and combos including them only occur if the series goes that far. Treat outputs as one input, not a guarantee.
